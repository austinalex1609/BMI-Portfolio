1. Evolution and Types of Pacemakers

Pacemakers have evolved from bulky external devices to compact, implantable systems. Modern pacemakers are battery-powered and can automatically adjust pacing rates.

Single-Chamber Pacemaker

This type has a single lead placed in either the right atrium or ventricle. It's typically used when pacing is only needed in one chamber.

Dual-Chamber Pacemaker

This pacemaker has two leads, one in the right atrium and another in the right ventricle. It mimics the heart's natural pacing more closely.

2. Electrical Safety in Pacemaker Use

Pacemakers must be isolated from electromagnetic interference (EMI). Design includes shielding, filtering, and fault detection mechanisms to prevent unsafe current levels from reaching the heart.

3. Regulatory and Safety Standards

Pacemakers are regulated by agencies like the FDA (USA) and conform to ISO standards such as ISO 14708. These standards ensure electromagnetic compatibility, biocompatibility, and battery reliability.

  • FDA: Premarket Approval (PMA)
  • ISO 14708-1: General requirements for active implantable medical devices
  • IEC 60601-1: Electrical safety standards

4. Safety Testing and Validation

Testing involves mechanical integrity, electrical output testing, software simulation, and in-vivo testing. Validation ensures the device performs reliably over time.
